A royal decree was issued appointing Professor Mahmoud Al-Sheyyab as a President of Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST) for the next 4 years starting on July 8, 2015.
Professor Mahmoud Al-Sheyyab, a professor in Pediatrics and Hematology-Oncology, is a prominent Medical figure at both national and international levels. He was also a former Minister of Health. Furthermore, he was the CEO of King Abdullah University Hospital during the period October 1, 2004 to November 1, 2010. He also served as Vice President of Jordan University of Science & Technology during the period 2003 - 2009.
Professor Al-Sheyyab has reviewed many studies and scientific articles in Pediatrics and Hematology-Oncology. He has remarkable range of important contributions to more than 30 scientific publications related to his field of specialty and has enormously contributed towards the advancement of Pediatric medicine in Jordan. [قسم] An KU Jordan-Netkite Project

[قسم] e Prof. Suhil Kiwan and Prof. Fahmi Abu Al-Rub are managing the

[قسم] ri et “NETKITE - Cross-border NETwork to foster Knowledge-intensive
— Sy le = business Incubation and Technology transfer” -Project in Jordan.

[قسم] of 0 t Ce Netkite is an ENPI project with ARCA- Italy as a coordinator, and
newidegesinssavesssmcests" — JUST-Jordan, CEEI-France, DIOGENES-Cyprus, ARST-Egypt, Hee PAUC-Palestine, and SUST-Tunisia as partners.
The overall objective of the project is to set up a cross border network, focuses on establishing new public and private partnerships, inspired by Open Innovation philosophy and using Living Labs approach, meant to support innovation transfer in order to make it functional to the growth and modernization of local economic systems and to the generation of new, qualified and sustainable jobs.
The specific objective is to enhance and improve innovation strategies and to establish an effective transfer process of innovation in the territories concerned, resulting on one side into the generation of spin-off companies in strategic sectors for the local economy, on the other side into creating pathways for introducing and managing innovation into mature productive sectors, creating new professional positions.

[قسم] Achievements
Seven Technology Transfer Officers (TTO) from different Jordanian organizations and institutions were trained for two weeks in different business incubators in France, Italy, or Cyprus. Jordanian entrepreneurs participated in a competition in Netkite with seventy innovative ideas. The evaluation process involved many stages including feasibility study, business model, and technology audit. Nine winning projects were selected and distributed for training in France, Italy, or Cyprus in Business Incubators. The training was for two months and during the training many meetings for the Jordanian entrepreneurs with potential EU investors were arranged. One of the projects was patented in EU and another is currently being patented in EU. A potential join venture between one of the Jordanian start-ups and an Italian company interested in renewable energy is expected within two months.
In November 2015, the best Jordanian projects will participate with a huge EU business competition where more than 50 EU investors will attend the competition to select some of the projects for investment according to an agreed agreement.

[قسم] New Medical Achievement at JUST (King Abdullah University Hospital)
One of the most specialized techniques has been successfully used at King Abdullah 7 University Hospital - JUST for a 2 years old girl, this girl was diagnosed to have choleduchal cyst type I which is dilatation in the common channel that transfer the liver products (The Bile) towards the duodenum, it is well known that this area represents a challenge to the surgeon because it is difficult to deal with due to its location, relation and physiological role, and the body usually accepts no mistakes in this area..
A laparoscopic resection of the dilated segment with the gall bladder was performed to this young girl and was reconstructed also using the laparoscope, this a well-known therapeutic procedure that consists of performing double connections, one between the jejunum and the common hepatic duct and the other between jejunum itself, to re-establish the transfer of gastric content in anormal way.
It is the first time to perform such surgery using the minimally invasive approach (the Laparoscope) at KAUH - JUST or around, the leader of surgical team Dr. Ziad Ahmed Bataineh says this technique usually performed at highly specialized centers worldwide especially for children.
Dr. Bataineh added, this approach is one of the most sensitive approaches and needs a high surgical skill in dealing

[قسم] with the small space inside the abdominal cavity using the laparoscope
